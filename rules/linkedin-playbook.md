# LinkedIn Playbook

Platform rules all agents must follow when creating LinkedIn content.
This file is read by the Writer, Quality Agent, and Content Strategist.

---

## LinkedIn Algorithm — 2026 Rules

The algorithm changed significantly. Old engagement signals no longer apply.

**What the algorithm rewards:**
- **Dwell time** — how long someone spends reading. A 45-second read without a like outranks a 2-second like.
- **Comments over likes** — substantive comments travel farther than like-heavy posts
- **Saves and shares** — "silent" actions (saving, sharing in DMs) are weighted heavier than public likes
- **Scroll depth** — did they click "see more" and read to the bottom?
- **Topical consistency** — the algorithm tracks your expertise and boosts you to relevant audiences
- **First 60 minutes** — LinkedIn tests posts with 2-5% of your network first. Only 5% of underperforming posts in hour 1 recover.

**What kills reach:**
- External links in the post body — 60% less reach. Link in first comment is also penalized.
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree") — actively downranked
- Generic AI-sounding content — LinkedIn's systems flag it
- Mass tagging — hurts trust signals
- Low dwell time posts — the algorithm learns your content is forgettable
- Editing a post within the first hour — penalized during the critical testing window

---

## Best Performing Formats (2026)

**Document posts (PDF carousels)** — 6.6% average engagement, 2-3x more dwell time than text posts. Each slide swipe counts as engagement. 8-15 slides is the sweet spot.

**Short native videos (30-90 seconds)** — growing 2x faster than other formats. Must be uploaded natively, not linked from YouTube.

**Text posts with strong hooks** — still work but must earn the "see more" click in the first 2 lines.

**Polls** — drive comments but only if the question is genuinely debated in the niche. Don't overuse.

---

## Hook Formulas That Work

- **Confession + specific number:** "I won't lie. I wasted 7 years doing this."
- **Contrarian one-liner:** "When will [common expectation]? Never. And that's okay."
- **Shared experience question:** "Ever get handed a new tool at work and thought... now what?"
- **Reverse psychology:** "This will be my least popular post."
- **Specific list promise:** "7 signs this [common thing] is a waste of your time:"
- **Insider reveal:** "What nobody tells you about implementing [tool/process]:"
- **I tested this:** "I spent 3 weeks testing [tool]. Here's what actually happened."

**Hook rules:**
- Specific beats vague — always
- Never start with "I am excited to share"
- The hook must earn the "see more" click — if it doesn't create curiosity or recognition, the post is already dead
- Don't start with "I" — it's the weakest opener on LinkedIn

---

## Post Structure for Dwell Time

```
LINE 1-2: Hook — pattern interrupt, curiosity gap, or shared experience
[blank line]
LINE 3-5: Context — why this matters or what happened
[blank line]
BODY: The substance — specific, tactical, from real experience
- Short paragraphs (2-3 lines max)
- Aggressive line breaks — white space increases read time
- One idea per paragraph
[blank line]
CLOSING: A question that invites a real answer, a lesson in one line,
or a specific CTA (not "follow me for more")
```

**Length:** 150-300 words for text posts. 8-15 slides for document posts (first slide = hook, last slide = CTA, each slide readable in under 10 seconds).

---

## Posting Cadence

- Optimal: 3-5 posts per week
- Minimum for algorithm consistency: 3 per week
- Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Best times: 7-10am in your audience's primary timezone
- Never post twice in one day — minimum 20 hours between posts
- Post lifespan: 48-72 hours when performing well

---

## Engagement Rules — First 60 Minutes

1. Do NOT edit the post after publishing (penalized during the critical first hour)
2. Reply to every comment within 60 minutes
3. Ask a follow-up question in your reply to deepen the thread
4. Like every comment on your post (signals to algorithm the conversation is active)
5. Do NOT share your own post to other platforms in the first hour
6. Do NOT delete and repost — the algorithm remembers

---

## Content Rules

- **Native video outperforms YouTube links by 3x** — always upload directly
- **Document posts replaced carousels** — upload as multi-page PDF
- **Text posts need intentional line breaks** — walls of text kill dwell time
- **No format beats a bad hook** — first line determines whether the post gets read
- **Images at 1080x1350 (portrait)** — this is LinkedIn's optimal display size
- **JPG under 500KB** — oversized images slow upload and may display poorly on mobile

---

## What to Avoid

- "Follow me for more" as a CTA — nobody follows from this
- Hashtags in the body text — put 3-5 at the very end if at all
- Tagging people who aren't relevant to the post
- Posting links to your own content in someone else's comments
- Screenshot posts of tweets or other platforms — low reach
- Inspirational quotes without original perspective
- "Agree?" as the closing question — it's engagement bait
