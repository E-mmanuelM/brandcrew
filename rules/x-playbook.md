## The Platform in 2026

X replaced its legacy recommendation system in January 2026 with a Grok-powered transformer model. The algorithm is partially open-sourced — more transparency than any other major platform.

Two feeds: "For You" (algorithmic, mix of followed + recommended) and "Following" (primarily chronological but increasingly AI-influenced). For You is where discovery happens — optimise for that.

**Critical 2026 reality:** X has moved toward a pay-for-reach model. Premium subscribers ($8/month) receive a 4–8x distribution boost. Non-Premium accounts can still grow but start at a meaningful disadvantage. For serious creator use, Premium is the highest-ROI investment available on the platform.

---

## Algorithm — How It Works

Grok evaluates every post on engagement probability across 15+ actions.

**Engagement weights (approximate, from open-source code):**
- Reply: 27x weight of a like
- Conversation (reply + author reply): 150x weight of a like
- Repost with commentary: significantly higher than bare repost
- Like: lowest weight — essentially a baseline signal

**What this means in practice:** A post with 10 substantive replies massively outperforms a post with 1,000 likes and no replies. Optimise for conversation, not likes.

**Early velocity is critical:**
- First 30–60 minutes determine distribution trajectory
- 10 replies in the first 15 minutes dramatically outperforms 10 replies accumulated over 24 hours
- Respond within 2–3 hours of posting to all engagement

---

## What Gets Penalised

- **External links:** Since March 2026, non-Premium accounts posting links receive near-zero median engagement
- **Engagement bait:** "RT if you agree", "Like for Part 2" flagged by NLP
- **Engagement pods:** Algorithm maps coordinated behaviour and flags clusters
- **AI-sounding content:** Grok evaluates originality — generic content scores lower
- **Spam-like patterns:** Too many posts in short windows triggers suppression

---

## What Works in 2026

**Threads:**
- Get 3x more total engagement than equivalent single tweets
- Accumulate engagement across all tweets — longer algorithmic lifespan
- First tweet must stand alone as a hook
- Link goes in a reply to the thread (not in the first tweet)

**Text-only posts:**
- Foundation of the platform — no special boost but no penalty
- Short, punchy, conversation-starting posts outperform long essays

**Native video:**
- Gets the highest session time — most valuable for the algorithm
- Even simple screen recordings or talking-head clips outperform text in reach

## Thread Format for Professional Niches

```
Tweet 1:  Bold claim or specific observation (the hook)
Tweet 2–5: Supporting evidence, real examples, specific details
Tweet 6:  Counterpoint or nuance — shows depth of thinking
Tweet 7:  Practical takeaway — what the reader can do
Reply to Tweet 1: Link to LinkedIn post or longer content if relevant
```

**Single tweet format:**
- Under 280 characters, punchy, one strong idea
- End with a question to drive replies
- No external link in the tweet itself

---

## Cadence

- Minimum: 1–2 posts/day to maintain algorithmic presence
- Optimal: 3–5 posts/day (mix of original posts, replies, threads)
- Best times: 8–10am and 6–9pm in your audience's primary timezone

## Premium vs Non-Premium

| Factor | Non-Premium | Premium ($8/mo) |
|---|---|---|
| Distribution boost | Baseline | 4–8x in-network, 2x out-of-network |
| Link posts | Near-zero reach | Some visibility |
| Long-form posts (4000 chars) | Not available | Available |
| Reply prioritisation | Standard | Prioritised at top of threads |

For a serious personal brand: Premium is worth it. The distribution difference is structural, not marginal.
