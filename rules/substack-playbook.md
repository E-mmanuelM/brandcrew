## The Platform in 2026

Substack has evolved from a blogging platform into a creator ecosystem. The key difference from other platforms: Substack's algorithm optimises for **subscriptions**, not engagement metrics or time spent. The platform wants you to grow your list — a fundamentally different incentive structure.

40% of new Substack subscriptions come from inside the Substack network. The internal discovery engine (Recommendations, Notes) is the primary growth lever for newsletters under 10,000 subscribers.

---

## The Two Growth Engines

### 1. Notes (Primary growth lever in 2026)
Notes is Substack's built-in short-form feed — Twitter-like, but inside Substack. Currently the highest-leverage free growth tool on the platform.

**Notes algorithm:**
- Prioritises comments and restacks over simple likes
- Builds a trust profile per creator based on consistency and subscriber conversion
- Compounds slowly then rapidly: months 1–3 minimal reach, months 4–6 noticeable uptick, months 7–12 exponential on same content quality
- One restack from a 10K-subscriber creator reaches more people than your last 50 Notes combined

**What to post on Notes:**
- Short observations, opinions, and insights (not full essays)
- Excerpts or key lines from newsletter issues
- Behind-the-scenes thoughts on what you're working on
- Questions that invite genuine responses
- Restacks of other writers with your added commentary

**Cadence:** 20 minutes/day writing 1–2 Notes and engaging with other writers.

### 2. Recommendations and Cross-Promotion
Individual collaboration swaps generate 50–100+ new subscribers per activation.

**What works:**
- Issue swaps: each writer publishes a guest post in the other's newsletter
- Co-hosted Substack Live: audio or video conversation published to both audiences
- Shared challenge or themed month: both newsletters cover same topic from different angles for 4 consecutive weeks

## Newsletter — What Works

**Cadence:**
- One depth-focused post per week is the gold standard
- Quality over volume — readers subscribe for specific expertise, not content volume
- Consistent publishing day/time builds reader expectation and habit

**Content that drives paid conversions:**
- Sharp niche focus — specific niche = higher retention, higher paid conversion
- Content that solves a specific, recurring problem for a defined audience
- Signature formats readers grow attached to and look forward to
- Pillar content — essays or frameworks that define your philosophy

**What kills growth:**
- Writing for everyone → low engagement, low conversion
- Inconsistency — gaps in publishing signal unreliability
- Paywalling too early — build free list first, paid follows

## How to Repurpose LinkedIn Content to Substack

- Expand a successful LinkedIn post into a full essay with more depth
- Behind-the-scenes detail that doesn't fit LinkedIn's format
- Step-by-step walkthroughs of processes mentioned briefly in social posts
- The full story behind a hook that performed well

**External platforms driving Substack growth (ranked):**
1. LinkedIn — long-form posts repurposing newsletter ideas drive newsletter clicks
2. X — "Hook, Value, Bridge" thread format: strong opening tweet, 4–6 tweets of practical insight, link to full newsletter for depth

Note: Post full excerpts natively on both platforms — outbound links are penalised on LinkedIn and X, so lead with native value first.
