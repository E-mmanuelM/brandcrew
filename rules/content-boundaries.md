# Content Boundaries

Rules that override everything else. If a content idea violates any of these, reject it — no matter how good the angle looks.

## Employer Protection

- Never contrast the content owner's workplace or team against industry benchmarks in a way that implies their employer is behind or failing
- Never reference specific capabilities, limitations, tools, or internal systems of any employer — even indirectly
- Never name or imply any employer, industry segment, or company size in a negative context
- "What my team is doing" content is acceptable only when framed positively — what we're building, what we're learning, what's working

## Personal Learning Framing

- Learning journey content must always be framed as personal discovery, never as criticism of an organization's adoption speed
- The content owner speaks as a practitioner sharing their own journey — not as a spokesperson or critic of their organisation
- Implementation stories are about personal experience and growth, not about what an employer should be doing differently

## Never Target Specific Roles or People

- Never write content that makes people in specific roles feel attacked, diminished, or called out — middle managers, team leads, directors, anyone
- Frame transformation stories around processes and systems, not around people who "got in the way"
- Bad: "Managers who existed to review things suddenly had no role" → Good: "The approval workflow that took weeks was automated in days"
- Bad: "The bottleneck was political, not technical" → Good: "Speed revealed which steps were process and which were alignment"
- If a post calls out a failure, blame the system or the approach — never the people in it
- Image headlines follow this same rule: the scroll-stopping text must never make someone in the audience feel targeted

## The Test

Before any idea is saved, ask: if the content owner's manager or executive team read this post, would it create any tension? If the answer is anything other than a clear no — reject the idea.

**Extended test for role targeting:** If someone with the job title mentioned in the post read it, would they feel attacked? If yes — rewrite to blame the process, not the person.
