# Anti-Slop Quality Rules

## What AI Slop Looks Like

Content that sounds like it was generated by an AI trying to sound professional.
Vague, padded, generic, inoffensive, useful to no one.

## Hard Ban — Never Use These Phrases

- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "It's no secret that"
- "Game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "Synergy" / "synergies"
- "Unlock your potential" / "unlock [anything]"
- "Dive deep" / "dive into"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Move the needle"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Think outside the box"
- "Best practices" (without specifics)
- "Robust" (describing a system or process)
- "Seamless" (describing any experience)
- "Empower" / "empowering"
- "Transformative" / "transform your"
- "In conclusion" / "To summarize"
- Starting a post with "I"
- Ending with "What do you think?" or "Drop a comment below!"

## What Good Content Has Instead

- A specific number, stat, or example in the first two lines
- A clear point of view — the author believes something, not just "it depends"
- Real operational details only someone who's done the job would know
- A hook that creates genuine curiosity or a pattern interrupt
- One main idea — not a listicle of 10 vague tips
- Specific enough that someone in the target audience thinks "yes, that's exactly my problem"

## Voice Authenticity Check

1. Could this have been written by anyone with a LinkedIn account? → FAIL
2. Does it reference something specific to the content owner's niche? → needed to PASS
3. Does it have a clear point of view? → wishy-washy = FAIL
4. Would a busy professional in the target audience stop scrolling for this? → if unsure, FAIL
5. Does it sound like a real person talking? → press release tone = FAIL

## Scoring Rubric (used by Quality Agent)

- Voice match (0-10)
- Genuine usefulness (0-10)
- Specificity (0-10)
- Hook quality (0-10)
- No slop markers (0-10)

**Pass threshold: 35/50**
**Max retries before human review: 2**
