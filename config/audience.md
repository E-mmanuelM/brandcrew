## Primary Audience

- SaaS founders and co-founders (seed to Series B) who are past initial launch but struggling to scale
- Product managers at B2B companies trying to improve onboarding and retention
- Growth leads at startups who own metrics but don't have big teams

## Secondary Audience

- Operators and chiefs of staff at early-stage companies wearing multiple hats
- Aspiring founders still in their day jobs, learning before they leap

## What They Struggle With

- Growing revenue without just throwing money at paid acquisition
- Figuring out why users sign up but don't stick around (activation gap)
- Pricing decisions that feel like guesswork
- Balancing speed of shipping with quality of the product experience
- Knowing which metrics actually matter vs. vanity metrics

## What They Want to Believe Is Possible

- That a small team can compete with well-funded competitors through better product thinking
- That there's a repeatable system for growth, not just luck and timing
- That they can learn from practitioners who've been through it, not just VC thought leaders

## What Content They Engage With

- Specific numbers and real examples ("we changed our pricing and here's what happened")
- Contrarian takes backed by experience, not hot takes for engagement
- Tactical breakdowns they can apply this week, not abstract frameworks

## What Content They Scroll Past

- Generic "10 tips for startup success" listicles
- Humble brags disguised as lessons
- AI hype without specifics
- Posts that are clearly written by AI with no personal voice

## Where They Hang Out Online

- LinkedIn (primary)
- Twitter/X (startup and SaaS community)
- Indie Hackers, Hacker News
- Niche Slack communities (SaaStr, Pavilion, Lenny's Newsletter community)
